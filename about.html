About LingoScope

The concept for LingoScope was first described in a Poynter article by Nick Diakopoulos: What data & algorithms teach us about the language news orgs use. The idea was to create a visual tool that could help us understand and compare the patterns, trends, and biases -- the frames -- in use by various news outlets.
By teaming up with Amy X. Zhang at NewsCred (now at MIT) LingoScope got real. It currently harvests data from 14 different news outlets and presents the statistical and visual comparison of data representing millions of news articles. A special thanks to NewsCred for making their data available for research purposes.
We've also applied the LingoScope to the study of moral frames in climate change blog discourse and compared how climate change skeptics discuss issues differently than climate change acceptors. An academic article on the topic was published at ICWSM in June 2014, here's the paper and citation:
  • N. Diakopoulos, A. Zhang, D. Elgesem, A. Salway. Identifying and Analyzing Moral Evaluation Frames in Climate Change Blog Discourse. Proc. International Conference on Weblogs and Social Media (ICWSM). June, 2014.
